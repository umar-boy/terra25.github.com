How Terra helps people

Terra has been observing the Earth for 25 years with 5 instruments: MODIS, ASTER, CERES, MISR, and MOPITT. This data helps to save lives, predict disasters, and improve the environment.

Forest Fires (MODIS)

The satellite captures fires around the world. The data helps firefighters and rescue services respond in time.

Floods (MODIS)

MODIS detects flooded areas, helping authorities to evacuate people and save cities.

Drought (MODIS)

The satellite shows areas of drought, helping farmers plan their crops and use water efficiently.

Air pollution (MISR)

MISR monitors smoke and aerosols, alerting residents to unhealthy air and helping to reduce emissions.

Volcanoes (ASTER)

ASTER monitors volcanoes, allowing for timely warnings to the public about potential eruptions.

Typhoons (ASTER)

ASTER helps forecast typhoons and storms, reducing the risk of destruction and loss of life among the population.

Global Warming (CERES)

CERES analyzes Earth's temperature, helping to plan measures to protect populations and ecosystems.

Fires in Indonesia (MODIS)

MODIS captures forest fires and smoke, helping governments respond and evacuate people in a timely manner.

Glacier Melting (ASTER)

ASTER tracks the melting of ice, which helps predict sea levels and protect coastal cities.

Vegetation and Crops (MODIS)

MODIS tracks plant growth, helping farmers plan crops and prevent hunger.

Carbon Monoxide (MOPITT)

MOPITT measures the concentration of carbon monoxide in the atmosphere, helping cities prevent poisoning and monitor air quality.
